Students

If you are a student, you can apply to become a volunteer at the Media Architecture Biennale and get registration and meals for free. As a student, you also have to possibility to participate in our 24 hour student design competition.

Become a student volunteer

Student volunteers are essential for the conference to run smoothly, as they will take care of for instance the registration desk, of being conference guides, and of running errands for the organisers during the conference. Prior to and after the conference, student volunteers will help set up and take down the venue, including packing conference bags, setting up/taking down the exhibition, and possibly help installing one or more of the interactive installations expected to be at the Media Architecture Biennale.

In exchange for their important work, student volunteers will receive free registration for the conference and free meals.

Being a student volunteer will give you valuable experience in the practical parts of running a large event, it will give you good network possibilities with the other volunteers from around the world, and it will give you a chance to experience most of the amazing talks and presentations at the conference.

Some student volunteers will be needed in the days leading up to as well as after the conference, while most will be needed during the conference days. In your application, please let us know how you expect to be available in the days up to as well as after the conference.

Applications for becoming student volunteer can be submitted to student [at] mediaarchitecture [dot] org and should be sent no later than October 5, 2014.

Student design competition

Like in 2012, the 2014 biennale will host a 24 hour student design competition prior to the conference. See the winners as well as all of the submitted concepts from the student design competition at MAB12 here.

The competition will run online and participants are not expected to be in Aarhus in order to participate: the design brief, the ongoing documentation from the groups, the communication between the participants and the organisers, as well as the final submissions will all be online, facilitated through social media as well (Tumblr, Twitter, Vine, Instagram etc.)

The 24 hour design competition will open with the online posting of the design brief and 24 hours later, the teams must have submitted their concepts, e.g. their video, process descriptions and final pitch.

Sign-up will open in the beginning of September 2014.
